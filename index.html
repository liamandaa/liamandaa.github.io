Projects

Johns Hopkins APL Internship

I have spent the past two summers interning at Johns Hopkins APL. I interned with the same group both summers, where I worked on penetration testing, data migration, and NLP projects. More specifically, I wrote scripts to sanitize Red Team attack logs, I wrote a web scraper to run sentiment analysis on social media articles, and I developed a Docker-based NLP tool using the BERT model to query the most relevant result from a database of more than 300,000 entries.

Geneva Research Internship

I spent six months interning with Dave Levin in his Breakerspace lab, where I worked on the Geneva project. Geneva is a censorship evasion tool that uses a genetic algorithm and packet-manipulation-based censorship evasion strategies to defeat nation-state level censors. I worked on configuring new servers in censored nations to expand Geneva to new countries, as well as writing scripts and analyzing packets to further to test censorship capabilities of new countries.

Technica

I worked as an organizer on the platform team for Technica, UMD's hackathon for underrepresented genders in tech. We used tools like Node.js, Vue.js, and AWS Lambda to create the platform used by 1,000+ participants in our hybrid hackathon. I specifically worked on improving the security of our platform by implementing route restrictions.
